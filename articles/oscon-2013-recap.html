OSCON 2013 Recap

Posted on 2013 Jul 27

I just got back from the 15th O'Reilly Open Source Conference (OSCON) in Portland, Oregon. This is my fifth year going, I think, and it was as fun as ever. This year, it was even more fun than usual because I got to see so many friends there. I'm now working at home with a distributed team and about a dozen of us showed up and got to hang out.

Image of my co-workers and me by O'Reilly Conferences.

I really like OSCON because it is a dream for generalists. Most conferences I go to focus on a particular technology, and OSCON is scattershot, covering anything as long as it is open-source, or related to open-source. This year, I was particularly excited to learn about Ansible, 3D Javascript programming, and Cascalog.

One very exciting thing about this year's OSCON is the expansion of talks about human interaction. During the half-day tutorials, there was one tutorial on game design, which I attended and thought was great., and another on using improv techniques with your team. Even more exciting to me was the session on depression. As a life-long sufferer of depression, I'm glad any time I see it openly discussed. It has a serious stigma about it that I think only open discussion will alleviate.

Image by bkerensa.

While I did not get to attend the session Bash as a Modern Programming Language, the slides are wonderful and should be of interest to anyone who is a programmer, especially on a Unix system.

On Wednesday night, I facilitated a Birds of a Feather session on roleplaying games and how they have been influenced by open culture. It was a fun time and I met many interesting people. We talked a lot about Dungeon World and the Old School Renaissance.

All in all, I had a great time. As always, I visited Powell's Books and have many books to read now. Thanks to O'Reilly Media for such a great conference and I hope to see even more friends next year. Here are the slides for all the talks, which contain a wealth of knowledge.
